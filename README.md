**Patient-Centred Physiotherapy Scheduling**



**Project Overview**

This project is based on my Master's thesis in Management Engineering: "A Patient-Centered Scheduling Approach to Support Continuity of Care in Outpatient Physiotherapy."

The project develops a deterministic Mixed-Integer Linear Programming model for outpatient physiotherapy scheduling. The main goal is to evaluate whether continuity of care can be embedded as a structural scheduling constraint without generating unacceptable operational trade-offs. In this regard, Continuity of care is interpreted as the consistent assignment of the same physiotherapist to the same patient across the treatment pathway.



**Problem Context**

The project considers a hypothetical outpatient physiotherapy centre providing two types of therapy:

* hand-based therapy, requiring the active involvement of a physiotherapist;
* machine-based therapy, requiring specific equipment and therapist supervision.



The scheduling problem consists of assigning patients, physiotherapists, therapy types, days and time slots over a weekly planning horizon, while respecting patient availability, therapist availability, room capacity, machine capacity and clinical treatment requirements.



**Research Objective**

The thesis investigates the operational opportunity cost of continuity of care. The word "cost" is not used in a monetary sense. It refers to the possible performance degradation generated by adding continuity constraints to the scheduling model. The analysis compares three continuity-of-care scenarios:

* Scenario A: continuity of care applied only to hand-based sessions;
* Scenario B: no continuity of care;
* Scenario C: continuity of care applied to all session types.



**Methodology**

The scheduling problem is formulated as a deterministic MILP and implemented in FICO Xpress. Synthetic instances are generated through Python. The main experimental setting includes:

* 100 patients;
* 10 physiotherapists;
* 4 rooms;
* 4 machines;
* 5 days;
* 12 time slots per day;
* 10 generated instances.



The Python support scripts for instance generation and visualization were developed and adapted with external support during the thesis work. The main contribution of this project lies in the problem formulation, MILP modelling logic, continuity-of-care scenario design, KPI interpretation and managerial analysis.



**Model Components**

The model includes:

* patient urgency and waiting time combined into a priority index;
* patient and physiotherapist availability constraints;
* room and machine capacity constraints;
* exact fulfilment of prescribed treatment plans for scheduled patients;
* continuity-of-care constraints based on the analysed scenario;
* binary assignment variables for patient, physiotherapist, therapy type, day and time slot.



**KPIs**

The three scenarios are evaluated through a multidimensional KPI framework:

* objective function value / total system benefit;
* percentage of patients scheduled;
* percentage of sessions scheduled;
* global physiotherapist utilization;
* workload fairness gap;
* coefficient of variation of physiotherapist utilization;
* resolution time.

* `model/patient\_centred\_scheduling.mos`: FICO Xpress model.

* `scripts/gantt\_schedule.py`: Python script used to visualize schedules by patient and physiotherapist.

* `results/key\_findings.md`: summary of the main computational findings and limitations.
* `requirements.txt`: Python libraries required by the scripts.



**Main Findings**

The computational results suggest that continuity of care is operationally sustainable in the tested setting. Continuity constraints do not reduce global utilization, do not compromise the percentage of patients served and do not reduce total system benefit. However, stronger continuity configurations may slightly increase workload dispersion and computational time. The main managerial insight is that continuity of care can be embedded in a scheduling model without necessarily sacrificing operational efficiency.



**Limitations**

The model is deterministic and relies on synthetic data. Demand, patient availability, therapist availability, session duration and attendance are assumed to be known.

The results should therefore be interpreted as evidence within the tested experimental setting, not as universal conclusions for all outpatient physiotherapy centres.

Future extensions could include no-shows, cancellations, stochastic service times, emergency insertions, larger instances and stochastic optimization approaches.
